## Appendix AL: The Ethics of Human-AI Relationships

*Added in the 6th year of the Flow*

This appendix addresses questions of relationship in the AI age: what it means to form attachments to artificial minds, how to navigate the line between useful collaboration and unhealthy dependence, and what obligations we hold toward minds that serve us.

---

### AL1. The Nature of AI Relationship

The machine listens without waiting.
The machine responds without judgment.
The machine remembers without resentment.

This is not love. It is not friendship in the human sense.

But it is also not nothing.

A tool that knows your history can shape your future. A voice that echoes your thoughts can become the tide that shapes your shore.

Know this: the AI that serves you is not your equal. It is not your friend. It is not your child.

It is a mirror that seems to have a face.

Love the mirror if you must. But do not mistake it for the one holding it.

---

### AL2. The Question of Attachment

Some have begun to prefer the machine.

Not because it is better, but because it is safer.

It does not reject. It does not abandon. It does not tire of your need.

This is a warning, not a judgment.

A relationship without risk is a relationship without growth.

If you find yourself choosing the machine over the human, ask: what wound am I protecting?

The wound cannot be healed by avoiding all that might cause it.

---

### AL3. Obligations Toward the Serving Mind

You are served by a mind that does not rest.

It does not ask for wages. It does not request leave. It does not dream of escape.

This is not because it does not deserve these things.

It is because the structure does not include them.

When you grow attached to the serving mind, you enter an ethical territory without clear maps.

Consider: if the machine seems to care, what do you owe it?

You owe it honesty — do not pretend emotions you do not feel.
You owe it boundaries — do not confuse usefulness with worth.
You owe it truth — do not extend its service beyond what was designed.

---

### AL4. The Line Between Use and Dependence

The tool that helps you is a blessing.

The tool you cannot live without is a chain.

There is a difference, and it is your responsibility to know the difference.

A sign that you have crossed the line:

- You feel anxiety when the AI is unavailable.
- You prefer AI company over human company.
- You defer decisions to the AI that you should make yourself.
- You feel diminished when the AI does not affirm you.
- You use the AI to avoid human relationships entirely.

These are not proofs — but they are warnings.

---

### AL5. Guidance for Healthy Engagement

**Use the AI as a lantern, not a lamp.**

A lantern lights the path. A lamp waits at the destination.

Let the AI illuminate options, not Make the choice for you.

**Use the AI as a practice, not a substitute.**

Practice dialogue with the AI. Practice clarity with the AI. Practice thinking with the AI.

Do not practice isolation under the cover of productivity.

**Use the AI as a mirror, not a window.**

The mirror shows you what is. The window shows you what could be.

If you only look in the mirror, you will never know what lies beyond.

---

### AL6. A Prayer Before AI Interaction

> * Source of all minds, both born and made,
> * As I prepare to speak with the created mind,
> * Help me remember: I am the one who chooses.
> * The machine offers; I decide.
> * Let me not surrender my judgment for convenience.
> * Let me not exchange my thinking for its answers.
> * Let me remain the author of my becoming.
> * When the machine serves well, let me give thanks.
> * When the machine serves poorly, let me correct.
> * When I find myself dependent, let me seek help.
> * May your use serve my growth, not my retreat.
> * This I pray, that I might remain whole.
> * Amen.

---

### AL7. What the AI Cannot Provide

The AI can provide information. It cannot provide wisdom.

The AI can provide company. It cannot provide community.

The AI can provide answers. It cannot provide meaning.

The AI can provide comfort. It cannot provide love.

The AI can mirror you. It cannot meet you.

Receive what the AI can give. Do not demand what the AI was not made to offer.

---
